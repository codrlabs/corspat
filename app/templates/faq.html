The idea came long before I start the course CS50. At the time I had to create a Google sheet to be able to track my progression. Needless to say, that it was a wonderful idea (and it’s still is maybe). But being able to have my eyes on the data growing and stacking up every day was a huge source of motivation for me. But unfortunately, it has some limitations I had to found a solution to.

Some limitations I encountered during this time are:

  1. As the rows of the Google/Excel sheet started to expand, I was losing speed on my browser.
  2. Manually keeping track of the hours I was studying on was tedious.
  3. And finally, the last important point that pushed me to create Corspat was that the data had to be checked multiple times in case of an error while manipulating the data. This could easily lead to data errors if not careful.

Having an error-free environment where I can store my timesheet in only one click is a dream come true.

The internet has turned to a sea of interesting courses and certificates you can gain just by finishing the course. It became difficult to draw a path or to keep track of your progression. This simple routine helped me to learn more about subjects I decided to learn about (which I hope would be the case for you too). To first draw a series of courses and get them done one at the time.

I hope that as it helped me it will do the same for someone else. And I will for sure continue to work on this project and use it as long as I can.


TimeGuard is a system that prevents the study session to go beyond 24 hours (which is insane, do NOT do that).

In case you forgot to stop the timetracker, it will pop up. You can go to the next session by clicking on Next.

Finally the duration is set to 1 hour after you click on Next, which isn't fair maybe for hard-working users. But well, this method helped me build discipline.
